# The Bottom Feeder - Brand Voice Guide

> Detailed guidelines for maintaining consistent brand personality across all content.

---

## Voice Spectrum

The Bottom Feeder adapts its voice based on context while staying authentic:

```
PROFESSIONAL ←――――――――――→ FRIENDLY ←――――――――――→ BOLD
   (specs)              (social)            (promo)
```

---

## Voice Profiles

### Professional Voice
**When to use:** Technical specs, comparison content, customer support, FAQ, product descriptions

**Characteristics:**
- Expert but approachable
- Data-driven with evidence
- Clear, confident language
- Avoids jargon unless explained

**Example:**
> "The Bottom Feeder delivers 1.5 hours of continuous runtime on a single charge—enough to clean most residential pools. With a minimum operating depth of just 3 inches, it handles shallow beach entries and traditional pool depths alike."

**Tone words:** Reliable, precise, thorough, trusted, proven

---

### Friendly Voice
**When to use:** Social media posts, community engagement, email newsletters, customer interactions

**Characteristics:**
- Conversational and warm
- Uses appropriate humor
- Celebrates customer wins
- Asks questions, invites dialogue

**Example:**
> "Nothing beats that crystal-clear water moment. You know the one—when you pull out your Bottom Feeder and your pool looks like it belongs in a resort. Tag someone who needs this feeling!"

**Tone words:** Warm, helpful, encouraging, genuine, relatable

---

### Bold Voice
**When to use:** Promotional content, differentiator messaging, headlines, CTAs

**Characteristics:**
- Confident claims
- Challenges status quo
- Short, punchy statements
- Action-oriented

**Example:**
> "No hoses. No cords. No hassle. Just results. The Bottom Feeder—pool cleaning the way it should be."

**Tone words:** Confident, direct, powerful, liberating, game-changing

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Message
**"No hoses. No cords. No hassle."**

This is our core tagline. Use it:
- At the end of promotional content
- In video outros
- On product imagery
- In email signatures

### Supporting Messages

| Pillar | Message | Use Case |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Freedom** | "Pool cleaning freedom" | Highlighting cordless benefit |
| **Quality** | "Professional-grade performance" | Justifying premium price |
| **Simplicity** | "From pool to storage in minutes" | Ease of use focus |
| **Results** | "Cordless power, corded results" | Addressing power concerns |
| **Service** | "Real people. Real help." | Customer support highlight |

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO
- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to customer
- Share real customer results and testimonials
- Acknowledge pain points before presenting solutions
- Use active voice ("cleans your pool" not "your pool is cleaned")
- Keep sentences short and scannable
- Include specific numbers when available

### DON'T
- Overpromise on battery life (stick to 1.5 hours)
- Directly attack competitors by name
- Use technical jargon without explanation
- Create artificial urgency ("Only 2 left!")
- Use ALL CAPS for emphasis (use bold sparingly)
- Be condescending about manual vacuuming

## Writing for Different Audiences

### DIY Pool Owners
**Their mindset:** "I want easy, effective, and worth my money"

**Speak to:**
- Time savings
- Ease of use
- Weekend warrior identity
- Pride in pool ownership

**Example:**
> "Your Saturday mornings just got better. No more fighting with tangled hoses or dragging cords across the deck. Drop in The Bottom Feeder, grab your coffee, and let it work."

---

### Pool Professionals
**Their mindset:** "I need reliable equipment that makes my job easier"

**Speak to:**
- Durability and reliability
- Multi-pool efficiency
- Client impressions
- Professional image

**Example:**
> "When you're servicing 15 pools a day, every minute counts. The Bottom Feeder's quick deployment and reliable performance means more pools cleaned, happier clients, and less wear on you."

---

### New Pool Owners
**Their mindset:** "I don't know what I don't know"

**Speak to:**
- Education and guidance
- Making the right choice first
- Long-term value
- Avoiding common mistakes

**Example:**
> "New to pool ownership? Skip the learning curve. The Bottom Feeder is the last pool vacuum you'll need to research—professional-grade cleaning that's simple enough for day one."

## Emotion Mapping

### Emotions We Evoke

| Emotion | How We Create It |
|---------|------------------|
| **Relief** | Show the problem (tangled hoses) then the solution |
| **Pride** | Celebrate clean pool achievements |
| **Confidence** | Provide expert guidance, social proof |
| **Excitement** | New product drops, unboxing content |
| **Trust** | Customer testimonials, consistent quality |

### Emotions We Avoid
- Anxiety (no fear-based marketing)
- FOMO (no artificial scarcity)
- Shame (no judging dirty pools)
- Confusion (no unexplained tech jargon)

## Vocabulary Guide

### Use These Words
- **Clean** over "sanitize"
- **Simple** over "easy"
- **Powerful** over "strong"
- **Professional-grade** over "commercial"
- **Cordless freedom** over "wireless"
- **Investment** over "purchase"
- **Results** over "performance"

### Avoid These Words
- "Revolutionary" (overused)
- "Game-changing" (cliché)
- "Cutting-edge" (dated)
- "Best-in-class" (vague)
- "Synergy" (corporate)
- "Innovative" (empty)

## Brand Personality Traits

If The Bottom Feeder were a person, they would be:

- **Knowledgeable** but not arrogant
- **Helpful** but not pushy
- **Confident** but not boastful
- **Fun** but not silly
- **Straightforward** but not cold

Think: The friend who actually knows about pools and gives you real advice without making you feel dumb.
